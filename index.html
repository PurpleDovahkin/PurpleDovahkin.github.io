Background Information - Cost of Suicide

Between 2007 and 2021 the rate of suicide in people 10-24 years of age increased by 62%1. According to the US Department of Health and Human Services more than 49,000 people died from suicide in the United States - that is the equivalent of more than one person every 11 minutes2. If we look worldwide, according to the World Health Organization, one person dies every 40 seconds and for everyone who dies another 20 attempt suicide but survive.

To put that in perspective,sing the Happy Birthday song in your head twice, in the time it took you to sing that, or brush your teeth, or text back and forth with a friend, or watch a couple TikToks, another person died from suicide.

With more than 134 people dying each day from suicide the stigma that surrounds suicide and open discussion of mental health and mental illness needs to be shattered. Everyone has difficulties in life - everyone has obstacles and challenges - everyone has mental health trials and tribulations - let’s normalize discussion of mental health - let’s normalize requesting help to cope with mental health troubles - let’s normalize individualized support plans for mental health. Those who are in need of increased support and assistance for their mental health journey should never have to feel guilty or less-than because they need compassion and understanding as they try to navigate life.

According to KFF (The independent source for health policy, research, polling, and news) teens under 18 have seen a death by suicide rate increase of 47.7% between 2011 and 2021 3. The National Institutie of Mental Health says that the pre-teen (8-12) rate of suicide has increased by 8.2% each year from 2008 4 and suicide has risen to be the second leading cause of deaths 10-14 and 25-34 years of age and the third leading cause of death for 15-24 years of age 5. This is a lot of numbers to throw at you - and when there are so many numbers it is easy to say it won’t happen to me or my loved ones - these are just statistics. But it can happen to you - never say never - because it happened to us.

My beautiful amazing loving funny brilliant gentle compassionate generous little sister Graciella Sawyer, known affectionately to us as Tavi, became one of these statistics when they were just 14 years old. They were one of 932 lives lost to suicide in Wisconsin the year they died. Our little family was decimated that day - none of us has ever healed and we will never be who we once were - we all live shattered and try to make our way in this world - which will always be colder and darker without Tavi in it.

This webpage is dedicated to Tavi - a truly amazing individual lost to the growing malevolent pandemic of suicide. It is also in honor of all of the beautiful children who have been lost to suicide and their loved ones who live each day with a part of themselves missing. Suicide comes at an impossible cost to bear - not only to the person who dies from suicide - but to their loved ones as well. Anything that can be done to reduce or stop deaths to suicide is a step in the right directions. If you are considering suicide or know someone who is considering suicide please contact 911 or the Suicide Prevention Lifeline or somewhere local Search Local Suicide Prevention Groups.
